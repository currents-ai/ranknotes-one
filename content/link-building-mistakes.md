Link building is still often misinterpreted in the realm of SEO. After examining numerous websites, I've identified common pitfalls that repeatedly hinder rankings.

## Mistake 1: Valuing Quantity Over Relevance

A single link from a pertinent industry site holds more value than many links from unrelated directories. Google's algorithm is adept at recognizing topical connections between websites.

For instance, if you run a SaaS business but have backlinks from cooking blogs and pet websites, it indicates manipulation rather than true authority. Target backlinks from sources where your ideal audience actually engages.

## Mistake 2: Overlooking Anchor Text Variety

A natural link profile exhibits a variety of anchor text. If 80% of your backlinks feature your exact target keyword as anchor text, it signals manipulation to Google's spam filters.

An ideal anchor text distribution includes:
- 30-40% branded anchors (your company's name)
- 20-30% naked URLs
- 20-30% generic phrases (e.g., click here)
- 10-20% partial match keywords
- Less than 5% exact match keywords

If your distribution deviates significantly from this, it's a concern.

## Mistake 3: Targeting the Wrong Pages for Links

Many sites fixate on link building for their homepage or primary revenue-generating pages, neglecting the rest of their content.

A more effective strategy is to build links to your top informational content, leveraging internal links to redistribute that authority across your site. This approach appears natural and reinforces your entire domain.

## Mistake 4: Ignoring Link Velocity

Links should accumulate slowly over time. An abrupt increase of 500 new backlinks in one month, followed by a dry spell for six months, appears unnatural.

Effective link building requires a steady effort. A few high-quality links weekly surpass the impact of an extensive campaign once a year. Google monitors these velocity patterns, and unexpected spikes can arouse suspicion.

## Mistake 5: Failing to Monitor Your Backlink Profile

Over time, links can deteriorate. Websites that initially seemed credible may turn into link farms. Adversaries can also engage in negative SEO tactics against you. Without consistent monitoring, these issues may go unnoticed until your rankings decline.

Conduct monthly backlink audits. Utilize Google Search Console and third-party tools to identify new links. Disavow any that appear spammy before they negatively impact your site.
